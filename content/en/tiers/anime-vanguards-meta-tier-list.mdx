## How to Get Vanguard Familiars in Update 13.5

Before diving into how these new Familiars shake up the **anime vanguards meta tier list**, it is essential to understand how to obtain them. The process revolves around completing specific portal challenges introduced in the summer update.

To secure these powerful upgrades, you must challenge the **Scorching Fate Trial Portals**. These portals scale from Tier 1 up to Tier 4. While playing through these standard portal tiers, you have a random chance to trigger a **Secret Portal**. 

According to community reports, clearing a Secret Portal guarantees a Vanguard Familiar drop at the end of the run. Because of this mechanic, grinding lower-tier portals to fish for Secret Portals is currently the most efficient farming strategy.

## The Rise of the Burn Meta: Fire Synergies Explained

One of the most notable shifts in the current meta is the heavy emphasis on fire and burn mechanics. Previously, burn units often conflicted with one another, sometimes overriding damage over time (DoT) effects or resetting stacks inefficiently. 

With the introduction of Update 13.5 Familiars, these limitations have been largely removed. Several units now feature abilities that scale their range, damage, or attack speed based on the number of burn stacks currently active on the target. This has led to a highly cohesive "Burn Meta," where running multiple fire-based units results in a compounding multiplier that melts boss health pools.

For players looking to push high-wave content, centering a team around fire-based damage dealers and burn-amplifying supports is now one of the most viable strategies.

## Detailed Breakdown of the 7 Buffed Summer Units

Each of the seven buffed units has received a tailored Vanguard Familiar that modifies their existing passive skills. Below is an in-depth analysis of how each unit performs with their new upgrades.

### 1. Leo (Mereoleona)
Leo has emerged as a premier damage dealer in this update. Her Familiar grants her a massive 800% damage boost, alongside a unique risk-reward mechanic called *Hellfire Incarnate*. While active, this mode reduces her attack range by 1% per attack but significantly spikes her damage output. 

Player experience indicates that placing Leo close to track choke points allows her to maximize this high-damage, low-range state. Furthermore, she gains *Alliance's Pride*, which buffs her range by 50% for 6 seconds under certain conditions, helping to offset the range decay.

### 2. Escanor (Pride)
Escanor remains the king of solo and AFK farming, but his new Familiar makes him a true powerhouse in team play as well. He now gains *Ultimate on placement*, allowing him to contribute high damage immediately. 

His *Ruler of the Sun* passive has been buffed, increasing the maximum damage bonus from his hour stacks from 30% to 100%. Additionally, his *Primal Blaze* passive inflicts an intense burn that deals 70% of his base damage, which can scale up to 95% depending on your active unit slots.

### 3. Newsman Forecast (Weather Report)
Newsman Forecast has always been a staple support, but his new Familiar cements his position on the **anime vanguards meta tier list** as a top-tier support. He can now apply his signature snail slow (which reduces enemy speed by 50%) and bubble debuffs directly to boss enemies. 

Crucially, his bubble debuff can now stack alongside other damage amplifiers, preventing him from clashing with other buffers on your team. He also gains bonus range for every burn stack active on the highest-HP enemy within his radius.

### 4. Quetzalcoatl (Sun God)
Quetzalcoatl functions as a hybrid DPS and fire-buffer. His Familiar provides a 300% damage increase and introduces the *Sun God Sacrifice* passive. If Quetzalcoatl cycles through different attacks without repeating them, he buffs the damage of all fire allies in range by 20% (this effect stacks but resets if an attack is repeated). 

He also inflicts extra burn stacks during his attack cycle and detonates all accumulated burn stacks for 130% damage when he eventually repeats an attack.

### 5. Hiei (Black Dragon)
In previous updates, Hiei was often criticized for "griefing" teams because his passive removed active burn stacks from enemies to buff his own damage. The new Familiar completely fixes this issue. 

His *Dragon Flame Absorption* no longer removes burn stacks; instead, it grants him a flat damage buff while leaving the burn intact. He also gains a 700% damage increase, and his *Black Flame* debuff is now applied permanently, spreading to nearby enemies when a target dies.

### 6. Ali (Ali Jin)
Ali Jin receives a staggering 1,500% damage buff from his Familiar. His *Major Burning Desire* passive allows his detonations and DoT effects to bypass enemy damage resistance entirely, while also applying a 50% slow for 10 seconds. 

However, his *Fire Manipulation* passive places his immediate burn detonation on a 15-second cooldown. This change allows him to build up massive burn stacks before unleashing a single, devastating burst of damage.

### 7. Habano
Habano is a dedicated fire-support unit. While her personal damage output remains relatively low, her Familiar increases her range by 30% and allows her to reapply active burns. 

She also provides a 50% damage buff to all units within her range for 10 seconds. Because she loses a portion of this range buff with each upgrade, she is most effective when placed early and left at lower upgrade tiers to maximize her supportive coverage.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Which Vanguard Familiar unit has the highest DPS in Update 13.5?
Based on player testing and community showcases, Leo (Mereoleona) currently boasts the highest damage-per-second (DPS) potential. When her *Hellfire Incarnate* active ability is triggered, her damage spikes dramatically, allowing her to shred high-health bosses faster than any other unit in this update.

### How do Vanguard Familiars affect the anime vanguards meta tier list?
Vanguard Familiars have shifted the meta toward elemental synergy, specifically fire and burn teams. Units that were previously mediocre or difficult to use (like Hiei, who used to remove helpful burn stacks) are now top-tier options because their Familiars allow them to work cohesively with other fire units.

### Can Newsman Forecast slow bosses with his new Familiar?
Yes. Prior to Update 13.5, Newsman Forecast (Weather Report) could not apply his snail slow to boss enemies. With his new Vanguard Familiar equipped, he can now slow bosses by 50% and apply his bubbled debuff, making him one of the most valuable support units in the game.
