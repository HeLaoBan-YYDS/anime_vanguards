## The Memoria Breakdown: One Above All

The introduction of Memorias has allowed players to customize their units with unique, passive-heavy items. For **anime vanguards escanor**, his signature Memoria—often referred to as "One Above All"—completely redefines his playstyle. 

This Memoria is designed for players who want to run solo or duo setups. It grants massive stat boosts but penalizes you if you place too many other units on the field. Player experiences indicate that obtaining this Memoria requires dedicated macro farming, but the payoff is undeniably worth the effort.

| Passive Name | Passive Effect Description | Strategic Value & Playstyle Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Lone Fighter** | Buffs this unit's damage by 100%. However, it reduces this unit's damage by 50% for every other unit placed on the map. | Ideal for true solo runs where Escanor is the sole damage dealer on the field. |
| **Above All Races** | Ignores negative status changes and weather effects. Treated as any category for stage-specific buffs. | Essential for clearing World Lines and stages with harsh environmental debuffs. |
| **Unstoppable Pride** | While at least five team slots are burned, this unit retains all upgrades across placements after being sold. | Allows for rapid repositioning (selling and replacing) without losing gold or upgrade tiers. |

### Choosing the Right Memoria: One Above All vs. Armored Mage

While the signature Memoria is incredibly powerful, it has one major flaw: it does not address the unit's vulnerability to shield mechanics. In high-tier portals, bosses often spawn with heavy physical shields. 

Because of this, community reports suggest that the *Armored Mage's Day Bayoff* Memoria is often a superior choice for team-based runs. This alternative Memoria allows his attacks to bypass enemy shields entirely, eliminating his biggest mechanical bottleneck. If you plan to run him alongside a full team of support units, swap to the Armored Mage Memoria. If you want to solo content, stick with One Above All.

## The Burn Slot Meta: How to Setup Your Team

To unlock the full potential of **anime vanguards escanor**, players have developed a highly specific strategy known as the "Burn Slot Meta." This strategy utilizes support units (most notably Rukia) to intentionally lock or "burn" team slots. 

By keeping five team slots burned, you satisfy the requirement for the *Unstoppable Pride* passive. This allows you to place him, upgrade him fully, and then sell and replace him anywhere on the map while retaining his maximum upgrade level.

### Step-by-Step Burn Slot Setup

1. **Equip the Right Team:** Bring Escanor (with One Above All Memoria and Blazing Solstice Familiar) and a support unit like Rukia who can trigger slot burns.
2. **Burn the Slots:** At the very start of the match, use Rukia's ability to burn exactly five of your active team slots.
3. **Place the Unit:** Place him down. Because of his Familiar, he will immediately have his ultimate ability ready to activate.
4. **Max Upgrades:** Spend your initial wave income to fully upgrade him to his maximum tier.
5. **Reposition as Needed:** If enemies bypass his range, simply sell him. Thanks to *Unstoppable Pride*, you can place him back down at the front of the track, and he will instantly reappear at max upgrade level with his ultimate active.

## Optimizing Stats: What to Roll on Your Memoria

When farming and evolving your Memoria, getting the right substats is crucial. While range is always helpful in tower defense games, his massive base range means you should prioritize raw offensive power above all else.

*   **Attack Stat (S-Tier or Higher):** This is your absolute priority. Because his passives multiply his base damage by 100% and 200% respectively, any flat increase to his base attack stat yields massive returns.
*   **Range Stat (A-Tier or Higher):** While range is secondary, having at least an A-rank ensures his massive line and cone attacks cover the entire map.
*   **Cooldown Reduction (Optional):** His base attack speed of 7.3 seconds is relatively slow. Rolling cooldown reduction can help him break enemy shields much faster, which is highly recommended if you are not using the shield-bypass Memoria.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Escanor the best unit in Anime Vanguards?
While units like Gojo and Sukuna offer incredible utility and raw damage in traditional team compositions, **anime vanguards escanor** is arguably the best solo and macro unit in the game. His ability to retain upgrades across placements and gain his ultimate instantly makes him unmatched for speedrunning and soloing portals.

### How do I get the Familiar for Escanor?
The Blazing Solstice Familiar is obtained by completing a specific questline introduced in the recent update. Players must complete these challenges and choose the Familiar as their reward, which can then be evolved and equipped directly to the unit.

### Why does my Escanor lose damage when I place other units?
This is due to the **Lone Fighter** passive on his signature Memoria (One Above All). This passive doubles his damage but penalizes him by 50% for every other unit placed on the board. If you want to run a full team, you should switch to a different Memoria, such as the *Armored Mage's Day Bayoff*.

### How do I bypass shields with Escanor?
Escanor's main weakness is multi-hit shields. To counter this, you can either equip the *Armored Mage's Day Bayoff* Memoria (which allows his attacks to ignore shields entirely) or pair him with fast-attacking support units that can quickly deplete the shield count for him.
