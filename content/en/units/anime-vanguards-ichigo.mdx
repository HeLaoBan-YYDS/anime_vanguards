## Who is Savior (Moonless)? Unit Overview

When evaluating the top-tier meta, the **anime vanguards ichigo** unit—officially named Savior (Moonless) in-game—stands out as one of the most mechanically complex and high-yielding DPS units ever released. Inspired by Ichigo Kurosaki from the legendary anime *Bleach*, this unit represents Ichigo in his iconic Bankai (Tensa Zangetsu) form, evolving further through his Dangai (Precipice) and Mugetsu (Moonless) states during combat.

Unlike standard tower defense characters, Savior (Moonless) is categorized under the **Every Group** classification. This means he benefits from every single group-specific buff in the game, making him exceptionally versatile. Additionally, his visual design is a treat for anime fans. Upon reaching Upgrade 8 in a match, he transforms into his black-robed Precipice form. Activating his ultimate ability triggers a stunning, custom animation where he unleashes the power of the Moonless form.

## Savior (Moonless) Skill Set and Passives

What makes Savior (Moonless) an absolute monster on the battlefield is his intricate passive kit. He is designed around non-stop, rapid-fire attacks and self-buff stacking. 

### Unique Passives

*   **Now You Feel Like:** When this unit performs a Basic Attack, he initiates an automatic follow-up chain: Attack 3 (100% damage) $\rightarrow$ Attack 1 (100% damage) $\rightarrow$ Attack 2 (100% damage) $\rightarrow$ Attack 4 (100% damage).
*   **Number One:** Whenever any unit on your team performs an attack that differs from their previous attack, Savior (Moonless) gains a 50% damage buff for 30 seconds (non-stacking). Additionally, performing a Basic Attack doubles his current temporary percentage damage buffs until the next Basic Attack. If he has no active buffs when starting a Basic Attack, he receives a flat 50% damage buff for 60 seconds.
*   **Shining Bright for Everyone:** This unit counts as "Every Group." His follow-up attacks completely ignore enemy damage reduction. Furthermore, whenever he performs a follow-up, he cleanses himself of all negative status effects and debuffs. For every unique attack an enemy is hit by, they take 10% more damage from his follow-ups. Finally, securing a takedown buffs his damage by 5% (stacking up to 50%) for 40 seconds.

### Combat Strategy: Handling the "Hollow Bug"

A common piece of player advice regarding Savior (Moonless) involves his Active Ability, **Moonless Sky**. Upon activation, you sacrifice lifestocks to deal a massive burst of damage (up to 1000% multiplier). However, community reports indicate that his transformed "Hollow" state can sometimes suffer from inconsistent DPS due to varying attack animations. 

To bypass this issue, top players suggest saving his Active Ability as a final nuke for the main boss. Keep him in his highly consistent Precipice Form for the majority of the run, and only trigger the Moonless transformation when you need to instantly delete a boss's health bar.

## FAQs

### How do I get the Complete Spirit Mask to evolve my anime vanguards ichigo?
The Complete Spirit Mask can be obtained as a rare drop (10% chance) from completing high-level dungeons. Alternatively, if you want to skip the RNG grind, you can purchase it directly from the event shop for 2,000 Coins.

### Does Savior (Moonless) count as a Cosmic unit?
Yes! Because Savior (Moonless) possesses the "Every Group" passive, he is classified under every single unit category in the game. This allows him to receive the 30% Cosmic damage buff from Deceiver (Monster Aizen) alongside any other group-specific buffs.

### Is the Savior (Moonless) shiny model different from the base model?
Yes, the Shiny version of Savior (Moonless) features a highly detailed model inspired by Ichigo's iconic Visored mask combined with his Bankai attire. It also features unique visual effects during his attack animations.

### Why is my Savior (Moonless) damage inconsistent after using his active ability?
According to community reports, the transformed "Hollow" state has highly varied attack animations, which can occasionally cause his effective DPS to fluctuate. For maximum consistency, it is highly recommended to save his Active Ability as a finishing blow for the final boss rather than using it early in a match.
