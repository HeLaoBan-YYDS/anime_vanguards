## Beginner Strategy: The Planet N Jinu Method

For those just starting their journey, the most recommended path involves Planet N, specifically Act 4. This stage features a boss named Jinu, who is a goldmine for early-game experience. Based on player experience and community reports, Jinu is unique because he doesn't just sit there and take damage; he spawns mini-bosses as his health depletes.

When you damage Jinu, he summons additional targets. In Anime Vanguards, each boss or mini-boss killed grants a specific chunk of XP. By focusing on this stage, you aren't just getting the completion reward; you are farming the internal XP drops from the summons.

### How to Execute the Planet N Run

1.  **Select Planet N Act 4:** Ensure you are geared for a quick boss kill but not so fast that you skip the summon phases.
2.  **Use Carry Units:** Beginners should look for units like Nobara, Alligator, or Agony.
3.  **Manage Health Thresholds:** Jinu spawns mini-bosses at specific health intervals (e.g., 75%, 50%, and 25%).
4.  **Collect XP:** Each mini-boss kill adds to your total. A successful run can net around 290 XP in just a few minutes.

## The "Leveling Loop" Strategy

A popular technique among the top 1% of players is the "Leveling Loop." This strategy focuses on the fact that Anime Vanguards rewards you for finishing runs rather than the total time spent in a stage. Instead of pushing for Wave 50 in an Infinite run, you focus on high-speed resets.

According to research into the game's reward structure, the most efficient way to build your base is to enter a stage with short waves, place a single powerful "carry" unit, and upgrade it just enough to clear Wave 10. Once Wave 10 is complete, you immediately restart. These short cycles provide consistent XP, Gems, and Gold without the diminishing returns seen in longer, more difficult waves.

### Steps to Master the Leveling Loop

*   **Pick a Short Stage:** Look for acts that have a low enemy count per wave.
*   **Deploy a Solo Carry:** Use a unit with high Area of Effect (AoE) damage early on.
*   **Automate Upgrades:** Focus on the first three upgrade tiers to maximize cost-to-damage ratios.
*   **Reset at Wave 10:** Don't get greedy. The XP-per-minute drops significantly after the first ten waves for beginners.

## Unlocking the Griffith Advantage

One of the most underrated systems in the game is the "Random Stage Event." By navigating to your settings and enabling this feature, you open the door to the Griffith's Eclipse event. This event can trigger on any stage from 1 to 10.

When the Eclipse occurs, the strategy changes: you should remove all units and let the event play out. This is the primary way to obtain Griffith, a unit that is vital for mid-game progression. Griffith can eventually be evolved into the "Struggler" Mythic, which is one of the best units for clearing high-XP stages. By cycling through short stages for XP, you are simultaneously increasing your chances of triggering this rare event.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the fastest stage to level up in Anime Vanguards?
For beginners, Planet N Act 4 is the fastest due to the boss summoning mini-bosses that grant extra XP. For advanced players, the first act of the Bleach Legends stage with the Champions modifier provides the highest XP yield per minute because of the boss-splitting mechanic.

### Does playing on Hard Mode give more XP?
Yes, playing on Hard Mode or using modifiers like Champions and Nightmare significantly increases the XP multiplier. However, you must balance this with your clear speed. If Hard Mode takes three times longer than Normal Mode, it is actually less efficient for leveling.

### Can I level up fast while being AFK?
Many players use **anime vanguards how to level up fast** techniques by employing macros or click assistants. By setting up a script to automatically place a carry unit and hit the "Retry" button, you can farm the Leveling Loop or Planet N stages 24/7 without manual input.

### Why am I not getting much XP from Infinite Mode?
Infinite Mode is better for gathering resources and testing unit limits than for pure account leveling. The XP-per-hour in Infinite Mode often drops off after wave 20-30 because the waves become significantly longer and more difficult, making fast-reset story stages a much better option for raw experience.
