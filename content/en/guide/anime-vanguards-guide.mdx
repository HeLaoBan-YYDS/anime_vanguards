## Getting Started: The First Steps in Your Journey

When you first launch the game, you will be prompted to complete a tutorial. While it is tempting to skip through the dialogue, pay close attention to the basic placement and upgrade mechanics. Upon completion, every player receives two foundational units: Nami and Zoro. In their base forms, these units are unevolved and lack specific traits, but they serve as the backbone of your early-game strategy.

Player experience suggests that your very first priority should be evolving Zoro. Unlike many other units that require extensive material grinding, Zoro can be evolved almost instantly using a "hat" item provided early on. This evolution significantly boosts his stats and lowers his placement cost to approximately $600, making him an incredibly versatile unit for almost any early-stage map.

## The Leveling Loop: Efficient XP and Gem Farming

One of the most common mistakes new players make is pushing into high-difficulty Story Mode stages before their units are ready. Instead, seasoned veterans recommend a strategy known as the "Leveling Loop." This method focuses on efficiency rather than completion. By entering a stage with short waves and a fast clear time, you can maximize your rewards per minute.

According to community reports, the most effective loop involves placing a single high-damage carry unit and upgrading it quickly. Once you reach wave 10 or 20, you should restart the stage. The game rewards you for the number of successful loops completed, meaning ten short runs are often more profitable than one long, grueling battle. This strategy provides a steady stream of Gems, Gold, and Unit XP, which are critical for the early progression mentioned in this anime vanguards guide.

## Essential Units: Who to Summon and Evolve First

As you accumulate Gems, you will need to decide where to spend them. The meta in Anime Vanguards shifts frequently with new updates, but certain units remain staples for progression. Currently, Moa is considered one of the most overpowered units for beginners. Moa possesses the Njutsu trait, which allows him to be placed at maximum level if you do not have other Njutsu units on the field. This "instant max" capability can carry you through almost any legendary stage.

Beyond Moa, your long-term goal should be Gear 5 Luffy. While the path to obtaining Gear 5 is multi-stepped—requiring you to evolve Gear 4 first using Firefoot and Skelly King materials—the payoff is immense. Gear 5 Luffy provides unparalleled AOE damage and utility, making him a permanent fixture in end-game teams.

### Priority Evolution Path

1.  **Zoro (First Evolution):** Use the starter hat to get an immediate power spike.
2.  **Nami (Evolution):** Grind Legend Stage 3 for Essence Stones. She is the best farm unit for generating in-game cash.
3.  **Moa:** Summon for him during event banners. His Njutsu trait is a game-changer for new accounts.
4.  **Gear 5 Luffy:** Focus on his quest line once you have a stable team to clear boss events.

## Advanced Resource Management: Rerolls and Essence Stones

Once you have a solid team, this anime vanguards guide shifts focus to optimization. Units have "Traits" and "Stats" that can be rerolled to increase their effectiveness. For example, getting a "Divinity" or "Monarch" trait on a primary attacker can increase their damage output by over 50%.

The best way to farm these rerolls is through Odyssey Mode. While Odyssey is more manual and difficult than standard story stages, it is widely considered the most "overpowered" farm for rerolls and gems. A dedicated player can earn upwards of a thousand rerolls a day by mastering the Ragna or Esganor Odyssey maps.

## The Griffith Eclipse: A Secret Progression Path

A unique mechanic highlighted in this anime vanguards guide is the "Random Stage Event" known as Griffith's Eclipse. By enabling this in your settings, you have a small chance to trigger an Eclipse encounter on any stage between 1 and 10. When this happens, the community strategy is to remove all units and let the event play out.

This is the primary method for unlocking Griffith. Once you have him, you can evolve him into the "Struggler Mythic" by sacrificing additional units and using his special ability. This is one of the strongest mid-game strategies available, as it rewards players for the repetitive stage cycling they are already doing for XP and Gems.

## FAQ: Anime Vanguards Guide

### How do I level up my units the fastest in Anime Vanguards?
The fastest way to level up units is by utilizing the "Leveling Loop" strategy. Enter a stage with short waves (like the first Story stage), place a strong carry unit, and restart the game after reaching wave 10 or 20. This provides consistent XP and Gems much faster than completing long, difficult stages. This anime vanguards guide also recommends using event panels to gain XP items and free Mythic units.

### Which starter unit should I evolve first?
You should prioritize evolving Zoro first. He is given to you at the start of the game, and his evolution is extremely easy to obtain using the "hat" item provided in the early stages. Evolving Zoro makes him a low-cost, high-damage unit that can carry you through most of the early and mid-game content.

### What is the best way to get Trait Rerolls?
Odyssey Mode is the premier location for farming Trait Rerolls. While it requires more manual play than AFK farming, it offers the highest density of rerolls and gems. Additionally, make sure to check the Raid and Dungeon shops, as they often stock hundreds of rerolls that reset periodically.

### How do I get Gear 5 Luffy?
To get Gear 5 Luffy, you must first obtain his Mythic version through summons. You then need to evolve him into Gear 4 using materials like Firefoot, Skelly King, and evolved Nami. Finally, you must complete the Gear 5 quest line, which involves specific boss events and accumulating a large number of Essence Stones. Using a maxed-out Moa can make these boss events significantly easier to clear.
