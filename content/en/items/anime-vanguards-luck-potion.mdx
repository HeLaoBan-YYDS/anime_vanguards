## Understanding the Role of Luck Boosts in Anime Vanguards

Summoning the perfect team is the core of progression in *Anime Vanguards*, and every player eventually looks for an edge to improve their odds. Using an **Anime Vanguards luck potion** is one of the most popular ways to tilt the RNG in your favor, especially when hunting for those elusive high-tier units. However, understanding exactly what an **Anime Vanguards luck potion** does—and, more importantly, what it *doesn't* do—is essential for saving your resources and maximizing your efficiency.

Many players mistakenly believe these potions are a "catch-all" solution for every type of summon. In reality, these boosters are specialized tools that specifically target certain rarities while leaving others untouched. By learning the mechanics behind the summoning system, you can stop wasting your currency and start building a more powerful roster of units.

## What Do Luck Potions Actually Affect?

In *Anime Vanguards*, there are two primary tiers of luck boosters available: the Super Lucky potion and the Ultra Lucky potion. Both items are designed to improve your chances of pulling higher-quality units from the game's various banners.

According to current game mechanics, these potions specifically increase the drop rates for:
*   **Legendary Units**
*   **Mythic Units**
*   **Exclusive Units**

It is vital to note that these potions provide a temporary boost, typically lasting for one hour of active gameplay. If you are planning a long summoning session, ensure you have enough potions to cover your entire window to maintain those optimized rates.

### What Luck Potions Do Not Influence

A common point of confusion among new players is the scope of these items. Based on [official Anime Vanguards documentation](https://animevanguards.fandom.com/wiki/Summon), it is important to clarify that **luck potions do not influence every rarity.**

| Rarity | Affected by Luck Potions? |
| :--- | :--- |
| Rare | No |
| Epic | No |
| Legendary | **Yes** |
| Mythic/Exclusive | **Yes** |
| Secret | No |
| Vanguard | No |

Furthermore, community reports indicate that these potions have no impact on the probability of obtaining "Shiny" units. If you are specifically hunting for a Shiny or a top-tier Vanguard unit, the standard luck potion will not provide the benefit you are looking for.

## Strategic Summoning: Pity Systems and Boosts

One of the most important aspects of the *Anime Vanguards* summoning system is the "Pity" mechanic. Pity acts as a safety net, guaranteeing a unit of a specific rarity after you have performed a set number of summons without success.

A crucial detail for players to remember is that **luck potions do not reduce your pity count.** Pity is a hard-coded progression system that tracks your total summons, independent of any active buffs. While a potion might help you hit that high-rarity unit sooner, it does not accelerate the countdown to a guaranteed pull.

### Pity Thresholds by Banner Type

To plan your pulls effectively, refer to the following table regarding guaranteed summons:

| Special / Selection | 50 Summons | 400 Summons | 25,000 Summons |
| Special Memoria | 50 Summons | 400 Summons | 20,000 Summons |
| Event Banners | Varies | Varies | One-time 20k |

## Maximizing Your Summoning Session

If you have decided to use an **Anime Vanguards luck potion**, you should ensure you are prepared to make the most of that one-hour window. Since these items are valuable, you don't want to spend half of your boost time clearing your inventory or managing your settings.

### Preparation Checklist
1.  **Clear Your Inventory:** Ensure you have enough space to handle a large influx of units.
2.  **Toggle Auto-Sell:** Go to your settings and configure your Auto-Sell features. This prevents your inventory from filling up with lower-rarity units that you don't need, which would otherwise force you to stop summoning to clean up.
3.  **Check Your Banner:** Verify that the current banner features the specific Mythic or Legendary units you are targeting.
4.  **Confirm VIP Status:** If you have VIP, remember that your gem costs for Special Banners are reduced, allowing for more pulls per hour while the luck potion is active.

## Advanced Mechanics: Shiny Units and Normalization

While the **Anime Vanguards luck potion** doesn't help with Shiny rates, understanding how to manage these units is still essential for high-level play. If you happen to pull a Shiny unit that doesn't fit your current build, you aren't stuck with it.

The Normalize Shop, run by the NPC Valentine, offers two main services for Shiny units:
*   **Shiny Transfer:** Allows you to move the Shiny status from one unit to another, provided they are the same type. This costs 20,000 Gold and consumes the original Shiny unit.
*   **Normalization:** Removing the "Shiny" tag from a unit will grant you 5 trait rerolls. This is a great way to recycle "trash" Shiny units that you don't intend to use in your main lineup.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does an Anime Vanguards luck potion increase my chances for Secret units?
No. According to the game's internal data, luck potions only affect Legendary, Mythic, and Exclusive rarities. They have zero impact on Secret or Vanguard unit drop rates.

### Will a luck potion help me get a Shiny unit?
Community reports confirm that luck potions do not influence the probability of a unit being Shiny. You must rely on your base luck or the Shiny Hunter gamepass to improve those specific odds.

### Do luck potions stack?
The game generally treats these as timed status effects. It is best to use one potion and wait for the duration to expire before consuming another to ensure you aren't wasting items.

### Does the luck potion reduce the number of summons needed for pity?
No, the pity system is entirely separate from luck boosts. The pity count tracks your total failures to pull a high-rarity unit and is unaffected by any items or boosters you use during the process.
